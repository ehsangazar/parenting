**[English]**

A sudden burst of vertical courage carries your child toward a distant toy, but halfway across the rug, they stop. A quick, piercing look backward confirms your presence, followed by a brief scramble back to touch your hand before they depart once more. This rhythmic pulse of wandering and returning is the hallmark of a brain mastering its first sovereign voyages.

![The touch-and-go refuel — the emotional battery recharged in a single contact](learning/raised-logic/l5-secure-base/body-opening-d8fa9734-9bcf-4037-b2d5-39076f675e09.png)

This "Run-and-Return" cycle marks the emergence of the **Secure Base**, where the parent functions as a portable charging station for emotional courage. While independent walking has expanded their physical range, their internal "Safety Map" is still entirely anchored in your proximity. This behavior is a sophisticated form of **Proximity Seeking**, where the child verifies the strength of the **Elastic Tether** before pushing their boundaries further into the environment. Every return is a moment of **Emotional Refueling**, allowing the nervous system to stabilize and gather the metabolic energy required for the next level of exploration. By treating you as a stable landmark, the brain is building the cognitive software required to balance curiosity with security, ensuring that autonomy never comes at the cost of safety.

### Predictable Availability
Maintaining a "Stable Geography of Presence" by staying in a consistent location while they explore allows the brain to finalize its navigational map. When the primary anchor remains predictable, the child can focus all their neural energy on the discovery of the room rather than the anxiety of tracking your whereabouts.

### The Auditory Tether
Using low, steady "Verbal Check-ins" to respond to their backward glances supports their need for connection from a distance. Mirroring their discoveries from across the room gives their brain the high-fidelity confirmation it needs to feel safe, effectively extending the length of their elastic tether without requiring physical contact.

### Honoring the Refuel
Providing a brief, intense moment of eye contact or a gentle touch when they return—without attempting to pick them up or redirect them—validates their need for a quick recharge. By allowing these "Safety Checks" to be brief and self-governed, you are confirming the validity of their internal compass and supporting their rising desire for independent range.

The distance a soul can wander is directly determined by the strength of the anchor it leaves behind. By celebrating the return as a victory of trust, you are providing the emotional soil for a lifetime of brave and purposeful exploration.

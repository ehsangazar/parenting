You’ve just emerged from a season of intense connection, where sitting tall and pointing at the world together became your shared language. It’s hard to believe how quickly the months have passed, but the foundation of **joint attention** you’ve built has prepared your child for a massive leap in how they relate to the world. You’ve mastered the art of following their gaze, and that simple act has laid the tracks for the symbolic journey ahead.

![Standing at the table edge, the room opens at horizon level for the first time](learning/raised-logic/l4-welcome/body-opening-a20ccf11-be8f-4e9f-baf4-7e8c46755d6c.png)

Your child is now crossing the **Symbolic Bridge**. Until now, their communication was functional—a cry for hunger, a point for a toy, or a reach for comfort. But now, the brain is undergoing a systemic shift where sounds are no longer just auditory noise; they are becoming **Labels** for reality. This is the birth of the **Intentional Communicator**. As their **Phonological Mapping** matures in the temporal lobes, they are realizing that a specific sound like "Dada" or "Ba" isn't just a vocal exercise—it's a social tool that unlocks a specific person or object. This symbolic shift coincides with a monumental physical one: **Locomotion**. As they begin **Cruising** along furniture and standing independently for the first time, their perspective moves from the floor to the horizon. Their world is expanding vertically and conceptually at the same time. They aren't just moving through space; they are beginning to categorize and name the things they find there.

This surge in neurological complexity often brings a temporary "storm" before the calm of mastery. You may notice a sharp peak in **Separation Protest**. As their **Object Permanence** matures, they don’t just miss you; they remember exactly who is missing, leading to more intense clinging. Sleep may also become fragmented again as their motor cortex "practices" **Cruising** or standing in the middle of the night. This isn't a regression; it's the high-voltage friction of a brain upgrading its operating system. They are more demanding because the environment is suddenly broader and more complex than their tiny nervous system can yet manage alone.

Your role is now **The Secure Base Interpreter**. You are the translator for their first clumsy symbols and the sturdy, predictable anchor they must return to after their brave vertical voyages across the living room. Your presence provides the emotional fuel they need to keep exploring this new, named world.

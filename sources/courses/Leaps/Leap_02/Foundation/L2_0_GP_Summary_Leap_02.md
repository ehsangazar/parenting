Standing at the threshold of six months, you might look at your infant and realize that the creature who once lay in passive receipt of the world has become something entirely different: an agent. A tiny, often frustrated, increasingly deliberate agent who has opinions about what they reach for, what they look at, and what sound they want to hear again.

![Two hands, two toys — bilateral coordination as a six-month milestone](learning/raised-logic/l2-growth-profile/body-opening-4f3890c5-02ef-460c-a4a4-54cbfa072cdd.png)

At the start of this three-month journey, your infant's world was a series of beautiful but disconnected fragments. Their physical world was divided by an invisible wall at their center, their visual world was flat and two-dimensional, and their actions were largely reflexive responses to internal needs. This was the stage of **Sensory Fragmentation** and subcortical dominance: the era of the "Internal Silhouette," where the world was something that happened to them rather than something they could change.

We have now reached the peak of the Leap 2 horizon, defined by **Midline Mastery** and **Accidental Agency**. The invisible wall at the center of their body has begun to dissolve through the maturation of the **Corpus Callosum**, allowing for seamless **Inter-Hemispheric Transfer**. Their visual system has been reborn through **Stereopsis**, transforming a flat image into a rich, volumetric world where distance and depth are finally legible. And through the power of **Secondary Circular Reactions**, they have discovered that they are a "cause" capable of producing an "effect."

As we look toward the months ahead, the storm of the **Cortical Shift** will begin to subside, giving way to a more stable and strategic "Active Mind." The **Perceptual Unity** they have achieved, the ability to see a mouth move and hear the sound as a single event, will become the foundation for their first truly intentional social interactions. They are transitioning from passenger to architect. You are no longer just a caretaker of their needs; you are the primary witness to the birth of their will.

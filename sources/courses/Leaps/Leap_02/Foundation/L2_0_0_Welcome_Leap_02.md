The last few weeks have brought you to a new threshold. You may have noticed your infant's fists beginning to loosen from their chest, their eyes following you across the room with a deliberate, tracking precision that was simply not there before. Something is shifting, and you can feel it.

![An infant tracks a parent across the room with deliberate new intensity](learning/raised-logic/l2-welcome/body-opening-1384859b-e610-49c6-9e86-f922953f026d.png)

The first twelve weeks were a masterclass in **Homeostatic Foundation**. Your infant's world was governed by **Subcortical Reflexes**, automatic responses that ensured survival while the higher brain centers remained under construction. Movement was a byproduct of these deep-seated neural circuits, and your primary role was stabilization: providing the external regulation that mirrored their internal search for balance.

We have now reached a profound neurological boundary: the birth of **Intentional Agency**. Between four and six months, the brain undergoes a massive shift from reflexive automation to **Cortical Control**. This Leap is defined by **Midline Integration**, where the two halves of the body begin to communicate across the vertical axis, and **Secondary Circular Reactions**: the stage of the "accidental repeat," where your infant may unintentionally strike a toy, notice the interesting result, and then exert immense neural effort to reproduce that action.

This surge in awareness brings a new wave of behavioral shifts, often appearing as a temporary "weather system" of friction. As the visual cortex masters **Binocular Depth**, the world becomes three-dimensional and infinitely more distracting. You may notice "distracted feeding," where a floor-creak or a passing shadow pulls them away from the breast or bottle. This is not a loss of appetite; it is a leap in **Visual-Spatial Calibration**. Sleep may also fragment as the body practices rolling and reaching in its quietest hours, a sign that the brain is prioritizing motor consolidation over rest.

Your role is now evolving from Regulator to Architect of Agency. By placing safe, high-contrast objects within the arc of their reach, you are validating their **Proprioceptive Discovery**. You are no longer just keeping them safe; you are building the laboratory where they first discover that their actions have the power to change the world. The next lesson begins with the most fundamental move in that laboratory: the first victory against gravity.

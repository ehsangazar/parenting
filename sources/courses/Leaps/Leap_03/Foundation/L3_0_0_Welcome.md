The last three months were a masterclass in **Midline Integration** and the slow transition from accidental discovery to purposeful reach. You watched as the "wires" of the motor cortex finally connected with intention, turning simple rolls and horizontal exploration into a laboratory of **Proprioceptive Discovery**. The floor was once a vast, two-dimensional map to be conquered, and your child has spent this time mastering the mechanics of the reach, transforming a reflexive body into a vessel of will.

![An infant rising to the vertical world — the horizon opens for the first time](learning/raised-logic/l3-welcome/body-opening-4002c396-92ad-496f-888b-4cc8bea7099d.png)

We have now arrived at a literal and metaphorical peak: the era of the **Vertical Pillar**. As the core muscles consolidate, the brain shifts from a ceiling-oriented perspective to a true horizon-view. This physical elevation is not just a motor milestone; it is the neurological birth of **Joint Attention**. For the first time, your child isn't just looking at an object; they are looking at *you* looking at the object. The world has expanded from a solitary 2D field into a shared 3D landscape. Sitting up allows the brain to triangulate social cues, transforming your child from a stationary explorer into a high-functioning **Lookout** in their own social environment.

This expansion of the social map brings with it a predictable shift in the emotional weather. As the brain becomes more specialized in identifying its primary caregivers, it begins a rigorous audit of the world’s inhabitants. You may notice an intense **Stranger Wariness** or a sudden peak in **Separation Anxiety** that wasn't there only weeks ago. This is not a backward step; it is a sign of increasing cognitive precision. Their world is no longer a collection of blurred faces, but a curated circle of safety. Expect sleep to ripple as they practice **Creeping** and vertical stabilization in the late hours—the brain is working overtime to navigate this new, upright reality.

Your role is now shifting to that of the **Secure Base**. In this vertical world, your child will venture out with their eyes and hands, but they will frequently "check in" with you for emotional refueling. You are the social compass, providing the navigational cues that help them determine if a new face or a new height is safe. By remaining a steady, predictable presence, you provide the scaffold they need to lean into this high-definition world, ensuring they never have to explore the horizon alone.
